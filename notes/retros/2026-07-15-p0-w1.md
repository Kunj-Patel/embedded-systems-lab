## What confused me?
Forward and backward copying didn't quite click but it makes more sense if you think of it as a sliding overlap problem. Going forwards you quickly run into an overlap where data gets corrupted, but if you move in reverse by the time you get to the overlap region that data has already been copied so its fine if it gets overwritten.

**At a glance:** Clarified the directional mechanics of `memcpy` vs `memmove` and how reverse-iteration prevents source-data corruption during overlapping memory operations.
